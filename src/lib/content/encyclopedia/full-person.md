# Full Person

The condition of a human being who retains, or has recovered, the complete texture of their thought, experience, and conviction. A full person holds views that may not resolve neatly into any available political or ideological package. Their positions were arrived at through genuine encounter with the world — through work, relationship, study, loss, and reflection — rather than inherited from coalition membership or shaped by the pressure of social conformity.

A full person is not without conviction, tribe, or loyalty. They are not a contrarian, nor someone who refuses to take positions. The distinction is not in what they believe but in how they came to believe it. Their complexity is an asset rather than a liability. They can be surprising. They contain contradictions they have not been pressured to resolve.

The Society holds that full personhood is not a natural resting state but something that must be cultivated — through sustained encounter with difficulty, skilled transmission of knowledge, productive disagreement, and membership in a community that makes the full version of a person welcome rather than costly.

The full person is both the Society's primary aim and its primary instrument. We are built by them and we build toward them simultaneously.
