# Hub

A hub is a physical place that members of a society organize around — a farm, a shop, a hall, a neighbor's property. The defining constraint is proximity: every member should be within walking or biking distance of at least one hub. No motor vehicle required.

This matters most in rural areas, where distances are long and fuel may not always be available. A society with well-placed hubs can function even when roads are difficult or transport is scarce. Members can reach each other, move goods, hold meetings, and coordinate work without depending on infrastructure that may be unreliable.

A society may have one hub or several. Where there are multiple hubs, each tends to develop its own character and area of focus, while remaining connected to the society as a whole.
