# Phronesis

*Phronesis* (φρόνησις) is the Greek word for practical wisdom: the capacity to judge well in particular circumstances. It is distinct from knowledge of rules or principles — a person with phronesis doesn't merely know what the rules say, they understand what the situation actually calls for.

This society is designed around phronesis rather than against it. The goal is not to anticipate every situation and codify a procedure for it. Rules that try to cover everything tend to produce people who follow rules rather than people who think, and they breed the kind of legalism that mistakes compliance for virtue.

Instead, the society trusts its members to exercise judgment — and tries to support that judgment by offering the questions a member might not think to ask. Not: *here is what you must do*, but: *have you considered this? have you asked yourself that?* Good questions open reasoning; rules close it.

A member acting with phronesis reads the situation, considers who is affected, weighs the near and the distant, and acts in a way they could honestly defend to the assembly. That standard — honest defensibility to one's peers — is more demanding than any rulebook.
